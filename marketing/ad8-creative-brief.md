## Why This Ad, Why Now

Ads 1–7 have hit four angles: genetic risk, convenience, weight/GLP-1, blood sugar urgency, before/after biomarker, and lifestyle belly-fat video.

What's missing: **social validation.** South Asian professionals are skeptical by default. They research, compare, and ask around. A testimonial from someone who looks and sounds like them will outperform a clinical statistic in conversion, especially for retargeting warm audiences who already know the brand.

This is the right ad for **Phase 2 retargeting** — people who've seen us before but haven't booked.

---

## Two Paths: Real or Hypothetical

### Path A — Real Patient Testimonial (Preferred, Requires Patient #1)

**Trigger:** After Sohan's first paid patient has a positive experience (ideally 4+ weeks in, measurable lab improvement)

Ask the patient:
- "Would you be willing to share a brief quote about your experience that I could use in marketing, with your first name and city only?"
- Offer: discount on next month, or just goodwill — do not offer payment (anti-kickback risk in any medical context, even cash-pay)

**What to capture:**
> "[Quote about: something concrete — A1c improvement, lab results, feeling heard, the convenience, the cultural fluency]"  
> — [First name], [City], [State]

**Consent:** Written authorization required. Keep it simple — one-paragraph release form.

---

### Path B — Hypothetical / Composite (Can Build Now)

**Legal framing:** "Representative patient story based on common patient experiences. Details changed for privacy."

Use a plausible profile that's consistent with who we'd be treating. This is standard in healthcare marketing when:
- It's explicitly labeled as representative/hypothetical
- No identifying details are fabricated as "real" patient claims
- No specific clinical outcomes are promised as typical

**Composite persona:** Priya reference not right — use a male SA name. Build:
> "Arjun, 44, Software Engineer, Bay Area"
> "My A1c was 5.9 for two years. My doctor said 'watch your diet.' I didn't know what else to do. Dr. Japa ordered fasting insulin — turns out my HOMA-IR was 3.8. That's where the real problem was. Three months later, I'm at 5.5 and down 12 lbs."
> *Representative patient experience. Individual results vary.*

**Risk note:** Hypothetical quotes carry FTC risk if labeled as a specific, named "real" person. Must use clear disclaimer. Never attribute to a "patient" without consent.

### Format 2: Side-by-Side Lab Result (1:1 or 4:5 — Alt Version)

Not a before/after body image (Meta policy compliant). Shows:
- "Arjun's numbers after 90 days with Agni"
- Table: HOMA-IR 3.8 → 1.6 | A1c 5.9% → 5.4% | ApoB 118 → 91

**Framing:** Lab card visual with subtle amber gradient border
**Disclaimer required:** "Representative results. Dr. Japa's typical patient experience. Individual outcomes vary based on starting point, adherence, and medical history."

### Format 3: Video Testimonial (9:16 Reels — Long-term)

**This requires a real patient OR a paid UGC creator.**

If patient can do a 30-second iPhone video:
- Casual home setting (not clinical)
- Scripted but natural — 3–4 sentences, personal, specific
- No before/after body comparison
- Caption overlay for silent viewing

UGC creator brief (if no patient yet): SA male, 35–48, doctor/engineer look, casual, authentic. Script centered on the HOMA-IR discovery angle — relatable, not scripted-sounding.
