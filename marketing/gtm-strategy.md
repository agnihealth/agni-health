# Agni Health — Go-To-Market Strategy
**Channel Prioritization: Social Media Pilot**
*Prepared: May 2026 | CMO Analysis*

---

## TL;DR

**Prioritize Facebook (with Instagram as secondary surface via Meta Ads Manager).**

South Asian males 30-55 are disproportionately active on Facebook. Meta's ad platform is the only one with sufficient demographic + behavioral targeting depth to reach this niche cost-effectively. TikTok is wrong demographic. Instagram alone is secondary but valuable via Reels for awareness. Run both on one Meta campaign — that's the play.

## 1. Demographic Analysis

### Where does our target audience live online?

| Platform | 30-49 yr US adults | Asian Americans | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Facebook** | 80% use it; 58% daily | 62% of English-speaking Asian adults | Highest penetration for 30-55 bracket |
| **Instagram** | 66% use it | ~58-62% of Asian adults | Skews younger; still strong for 35-44 |
| **TikTok** | ~35% use it | Lower than general pop for 35+ | 18-29 dominated; wrong demo |

**Key insight:** Facebook is the dominant platform for South Asian males 30-55. The 50-64 bracket shows 54% daily Facebook usage. Asian American adults are *more* likely to click on social media ads than the general US population — a conversion advantage we can exploit.

South Asian immigrant communities (30-55 cohort) are active in Facebook Groups (desi community groups, professional networks), consume Hindi/South Asian language content, and share health content via Facebook. This community also connects Facebook to WhatsApp share chains — organic amplification potential.

**TikTok verdict: Eliminate from initial pilot.** Less than 20% of 45-54 year olds use it actively. Competitor content for metabolic health on TikTok targets 20-35 year olds doing ozempic content. Not our patient.

## 2. Ad Platform Capabilities

### Meta (Facebook + Instagram) — Recommended Platform

**Why Meta wins for this audience:**

- **Language targeting:** Hindi, Gujarati, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu — target by browser/device language. Reaches first-gen immigrants precisely.
- **Interest proxies:** Bollywood, cricket, South Asian cuisine, Indian cooking, Diwali, Ayurveda, yoga (Indian origin) — layered together these create a strong South Asian affinity audience without needing ethnic targeting.
- **Behavioral signals:** "Likely to engage with health content," fitness interests, health condition awareness interests (prediabetes, diabetes management, weight loss).
- **Custom Audiences:** Upload CRM/waitlist emails → lookalike audience. Scale fast once seeded.
- **Lead Forms:** Native Facebook/Instagram lead gen forms. Patient completes intake without leaving the app — significantly reduces drop-off vs. redirect to landing page.
- **Age + Gender filter:** Pinpoint 30-55, male.
- **Geo-targeting:** South Asian population density is highest in: NY metro, Bay Area, Houston, Chicago, NJ, Atlanta, Dallas, Seattle. Target these DMAs first.

**Note on ethnic affinity targeting:** Meta removed formal ethnic affinity segments in 2022 (civil rights settlement). However, the interest/language layering above achieves similar precision without policy risk.

### TikTok — Pass for Now

Weak demographic overlap, limited interest-based health targeting for older segments, and a creative format (short-form viral video) that doesn't fit trust-building for a medical service. Revisit in 12-18 months if brand awareness is established.

Based on WordStream 2025 benchmark data (1,000+ campaigns) and healthcare vertical analysis:

| Metric | Healthcare (Meta) | Our Estimate (Niche SA audience) |
|---|---|---|
| **CPM** | $10–15 | $12–18 (niche = slightly higher) |
| **CPC (Traffic)** | $1.20–2.00 | $1.50–2.50 |
| **CPC (Lead Gen)** | $0.80–1.50 | $1.00–2.00 |
| **CTR** | 1.2–1.8% | 1.5–2.5% (higher — targeted messaging) |
| **CPL (Lead Form)** | $15–50 | $10–25 (high relevance = better conversion) |

**Advantage vs. Google:** Meta Lead Ads CPL runs 40-60% cheaper than Google Search for healthcare. For a new brand, Facebook lets us buy attention and build the funnel before Google captures high-intent searchers (who often don't exist yet for an unknown brand).

**Instagram-specific:** Reels CPM is currently 20-30% cheaper than Facebook Feed due to supply surplus. Use Reels for awareness at low cost, Facebook feed/Lead Ads for conversion.

## 4. Content Fit — What Works for Metabolic Health Messaging

### What resonates for this audience:
- **Authority without condescension.** South Asian professionals (engineers, doctors, business owners) don't respond to dumbed-down health content. Speak to their intelligence.
- **Cultural specificity over generic.** "South Asians develop diabetes at lower BMI levels" is more compelling than "Are you at risk?" Generic health ads get ignored; culturally specific ones stop thumbs.
- **No shame, no fear.** Fear-based weight loss messaging underperforms with this demo and risks Meta policy flags. Use empowerment framing.
- **Physician credibility.** This audience trusts doctors. Show the physician behind the service. South Asian doctor on the team? Feature them prominently.
- **Practical, outcome-oriented.** "See results in 90 days" > "Start your journey." This demo tracks metrics, not feelings.

### Format recommendations:
- **Facebook Feed (single image):** Lead gen campaigns. Clear CTA → "Check Your Eligibility."
- **Facebook Carousel:** Educational content (3-5 cards explaining metabolic risk, services, pricing). Works for retargeting.
- **Instagram Reels (15-30s):** Awareness. Soft CTA → "Learn more." Drive to profile or link in bio.
- **Instagram Story:** Flash offer, urgency, swipe-up to lead form.

## 6. Channel Recommendation

### ✅ RECOMMENDATION: META (Facebook primary, Instagram secondary)

Run as unified Meta campaign via Ads Manager. Facebook for lead capture; Instagram Reels for brand awareness.

**Why not Instagram-only:** Instagram's 35-44 penetration is lower than Facebook for this demo. Facebook has better lead form infrastructure. But Instagram Reels CPM is cheaper for impressions — use it for top-of-funnel.

**Why not TikTok:** Wrong age demo. Wrong creative format for medical trust. Not where South Asian 40-year-old engineers are.

**Proof point from competitors:** Mochi Health, one of the fastest-growing GLP-1 telehealth startups, has leaned into Facebook as primary channel specifically because of lead form efficiency in the healthcare space.

## 7. Hero Image Concepts

Three existing images mapped to campaign objectives:

---

### Concept 1: "Morning Window" → Facebook Feed Lead Ad

**Campaign objective:** Lead generation  
**Audience:** Retargeting + lookalike of engaged users

**Visual direction:**
> Man, 40s, South Asian features, standing at a window in morning light. Clean, modern home. Phone in hand — health app visible on screen (HbA1c or glucose reading). Expression: calm, controlled, not anxious. Overlay text: "Your morning just got clearer."

**Rationale:** Morning is when South Asians check blood sugar. The image creates instant recognition — "that's me." The phone/health app detail signals digital-native healthcare. The mood is aspirational, not clinical. Strong for lead gen where we want an emotional pause-and-click.

**CTA:** "Check if Agni Health is right for you →"

---

### Concept 2: "Outdoor Walk" → Instagram Reels Thumbnail / Awareness

**Campaign objective:** Awareness, reach  
**Audience:** Cold South Asian male 30-55 audience in target DMAs

**Visual direction:**
> Man, South Asian, early 50s, walking in a suburban or urban park. Morning or golden hour light. Comfortable clothes, not gym gear. Relaxed gait — this is his daily walk, not a workout. Optional: subtle caption overlay "After 90 days with Agni."

**Rationale:** Walking is the #1 lifestyle intervention recommended to South Asian patients for metabolic health. This image signals "this is accessible — you don't need to become an athlete." The "after 90 days" framing implies transformation without showing a dramatic before/after (which Meta flags). Perfect for Reels where autoplay captures mid-scroll.

**CTA:** "See how it works" → link to explainer page

---

### Concept 3: "Kitchen + Tech" → Instagram Carousel / Facebook Carousel Retargeting

**Campaign objective:** Consideration, retargeting  
**Audience:** Website visitors, video viewers, people who engaged with Concept 1 or 2

**Visual direction:**
> Man, South Asian, in a modern kitchen. Cooking (or just cooked — pot on stove). Tablet or phone on counter showing Agni Health app or consultation interface. No guilt, no "bad food" imagery — the food is good, the context is healthy. Overlay: "Your diet doesn't have to change. Your doctor does."

**Rationale:** South Asian food culture is central to identity — any messaging that implies you must abandon your food will be rejected. This image says: "we work with your lifestyle, not against it." The tech element (tablet/app) reinforces telehealth convenience. Ideal for carousel card 1 in a retargeting sequence where cards 2-4 explain the service.

**CTA:** "Book a free consultation →"

## 8. Ad Copy Variations

### Variation A — Pain-Aware / Direct

**Format:** Facebook Feed, Lead Ad  
**Audience:** Cold, interest-targeted (diabetes awareness, prediabetes, Indian health)

> **Headline:** Your doctor mentioned prediabetes. Now what?
>
> **Body:** South Asian men are 3–4× more likely to develop type 2 diabetes — often at a healthy weight. Agni Health connects you with a physician who understands your biology in under 48 hours. No referrals. No waiting rooms. First consultation is free.
>
> **CTA:** Check Your Eligibility

*Why it works:* Opens with a moment of recognition (the "now what?" feeling after a diagnosis). Names the biological reality without shaming. Removes friction ("no referrals, no wait"). The free consultation eliminates cost as a barrier to clicking.

---

### Variation B — Cultural Identity / Differentiated

**Format:** Instagram Feed or Story  
**Audience:** Cold South Asian male 30-55, language-targeted (Hindi/South Asian)

> **Headline:** Built for how South Asian bodies actually work.
>
> **Body:** Most weight loss programs were built for Western bodies. South Asians have different metabolic profiles — higher insulin resistance at lower BMI, different risk thresholds. Agni Health is the first telehealth clinic designed with your genetics in mind. Evidence-based. Culturally aware. Starting this week.
>
> **CTA:** See If You Qualify

*Why it works:* Appeals to the intelligence of a professional audience that has felt unseen by generic healthcare. Positions Agni as a category of one — not just another weight loss clinic. "Culturally aware" is a signal phrase this demographic responds to without being tokenizing.

---

### Variation C — Aspirational / Future Self

**Format:** Facebook Carousel or Video (15s), Awareness + Retargeting  
**Audience:** Retargeting — anyone who visited site or watched 50%+ of a previous video

> **Headline:** What if your 50s were your healthiest decade?
>
> **Body:** GLP-1 medications. Continuous glucose monitoring. A physician who knows that a 28 BMI can still mean metabolic syndrome if you're South Asian. Agni Health brings all of it together — from your phone, on your schedule. See what's possible.
>
> **CTA:** Schedule a Free Consultation

*Why it works:* Reframes the age 40-50 window as opportunity, not decline. Name-drops specific interventions (GLP-1, CGM) to signal medical seriousness to a research-oriented audience. The "28 BMI" detail is a pattern interrupt — it tells them we know something their last doctor didn't.

## 10. Launch Sequence

**Week 1:** Awareness only. Run Concept 2 (Outdoor Walk) + Variation B copy on Instagram Reels + Facebook. Goal: build pixel data, seed retargeting pool.

**Week 2:** Layer in Lead Ads. Run Concept 1 (Morning Window) + Variation A on Facebook Lead Forms. Let both awareness and lead gen run simultaneously.

**Week 3:** Retargeting kicks in. Run Concept 3 (Kitchen + Tech) + Variation C to warm audience. This audience is already familiar with Agni — conversion copy can be more direct.

**Week 4:** Kill bottom 40% of ad sets by CPL. Double down on top performer. Prepare lookalike audience from lead form submissions for Month 2 scale.

## Appendix: Why Not Google Search?

Google Search is a high-intent channel — great for Month 2+ when people are actively searching "South Asian diabetes doctor near me." Problem: unknown brand + niche query = low search volume. You'll spend $80-150/click on healthcare keywords with no brand recognition to convert them.

Start with Meta to build awareness and a patient base. Then activate Google Search retargeting + branded search in Month 2 when you have a pixel audience and some social proof.
