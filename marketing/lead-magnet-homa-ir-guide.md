### Page 2: The Three Numbers

**Number 1: HOMA-IR**  
*What it is:* The best available measure of insulin resistance.  
*How it's calculated:* (Fasting Insulin × Fasting Glucose) ÷ 405  
*Why it matters for you:* A score of 2.0 may look "borderline normal" in Western data — but in a South Asian man with family history and a waist >90cm, it's a red flag worth acting on.  
*What to ask for:* "I'd like fasting insulin added to my next blood draw." CPT code: 83525.

| HOMA-IR | Interpretation |
|---------|----------------|
| <1.0 | Excellent insulin sensitivity |
| 1.0–1.9 | Normal |
| 2.0–2.9 | Early insulin resistance |
| 3.0–4.9 | Significant — intervention indicated |
| ≥5.0 | Severe |

---

**Number 2: ApoB**  
*What it is:* A direct measure of atherogenic particle count — more accurate than LDL for South Asians.  
*Why standard LDL misses SA risk:* SA men tend to have more small, dense LDL particles (high ApoB) with a "normal" calculated LDL. The particles are what cause plaques, not the cholesterol they carry.  
*Target for SA men:* ApoB < 80 mg/dL (more conservative than general population)  
*What to ask for:* "Can you add an ApoB to my lipid panel?" Most labs process it from the same blood draw.

---

**Number 3: Waist Circumference**  
*What it is:* The simplest measure of visceral fat — the metabolically dangerous fat type.  
*The SA threshold:* >90 cm (about 35.4 inches) for men = metabolically elevated risk. The standard US threshold is 102 cm (40 inches) — designed for European-origin populations.  
*How to measure:* Tape measure at the level of your navel, after exhaling normally.
