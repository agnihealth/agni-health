# Getting Your Medication — Agni Health Patient Guide

Your provider will send your prescription electronically. Below are your options for filling it, from lowest to highest cost.

---

## Option 1: Insurance + Manufacturer Savings Card
**Best if you have commercial insurance (not Medicare/Medicaid)**

Most insurance plans cover GLP-1 medications for **type 2 diabetes** (Ozempic, Mounjaro). Coverage for obesity/weight loss is less common but worth checking.

**Steps:**
1. Check your formulary at your insurer's website
2. If covered, use a manufacturer savings card to reduce your copay:
   - **Ozempic/Wegovy** → [novocare.com/savings](https://www.novocare.com) — as low as $25/mo
   - **Mounjaro** → [lillycares.com](https://www.lillycares.com) — as low as $25/mo with commercial insurance

> Your Agni Health provider can supply a prior authorization letter if your insurer requires one.

---

## Option 2: Manufacturer Direct Pharmacy (Cash-Pay)
**Best if uninsured or insurance doesn't cover your medication**

Both Novo Nordisk and Eli Lilly sell directly to patients at significantly reduced cash prices — no insurance needed.

| Medication | Program | Monthly Cost |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| Ozempic / Wegovy (injection) | NovoCare Pharmacy | $349/mo most doses |
| Wegovy (oral pill) | NovoCare Pharmacy | $149/mo |
| Zepbound (tirzepatide) | LillyDirect | $299–449/mo |
| Mounjaro (tirzepatide) | LillyDirect | $349–499/mo |

- **NovoCare:** [novocare.com](https://www.novocare.com)
- **LillyDirect:** [lillydirect.com](https://www.lillydirect.com) — pickup available at Walmart pharmacies

---

## Option 3: Retail Pharmacy (Cash-Pay)
**Generally the most expensive — use only if Options 1 and 2 don't work**

List prices without insurance run $900–1,350/mo. Use GoodRx or Cost Plus Drugs to compare.

- **Metformin** (if prescribed): ~$4–10/mo at any pharmacy
