### 1. What is Telehealth?

Telehealth involves the delivery of healthcare services using electronic communications, including:
- **Video conferencing** (real-time video and audio)
- **Secure messaging** (asynchronous text-based communication)
- **Remote patient monitoring** (when applicable)
- **Electronic transmission** of medical records, prescriptions, and lab orders

Your care at Agni Health will primarily be delivered through these methods rather than traditional in-person visits.

---

### 2. Benefits of Telehealth

Telehealth may provide benefits including:
- Improved access to care regardless of geographic location
- Reduced travel time and associated costs
- Convenience of receiving care from home or work
- More frequent touchpoints for ongoing management
- Timely follow-up without scheduling delays

---

### 3. Limitations and Risks of Telehealth

You understand that telehealth has limitations compared to in-person care:

**Clinical Limitations:**
- Physical examination is limited (no vital signs, palpation, auscultation unless you have home equipment)
- Some conditions cannot be adequately evaluated or treated via telehealth
- Diagnosis may require in-person evaluation, lab work, or imaging
- Your provider may determine telehealth is not appropriate for your condition and refer you for in-person care

**Technical Risks:**
- Technology may fail (internet outages, software glitches, device malfunctions)
- Audio or video quality may be poor
- Sessions may be interrupted unexpectedly
- Security breaches, while rare, are possible despite safeguards

**Other Considerations:**
- Response time may be delayed for asynchronous messaging (not real-time)
- Emergency services cannot be dispatched through telehealth

---

### 4. Emergency Situations

**TELEHEALTH IS NOT FOR EMERGENCIES.**

If you experience any of the following, **CALL 911 OR GO TO YOUR NEAREST EMERGENCY ROOM:**
- Chest pain or pressure
- Difficulty breathing
- Signs of stroke (sudden numbness, confusion, trouble speaking, severe headache)
- Severe allergic reaction
- Suicidal thoughts or intentions
- Any condition you believe is life-threatening

Agni Health cannot dispatch emergency services and is not staffed for immediate emergency response.

---

### 5. Technology Requirements

To participate in telehealth visits, you need:
- A device with a camera and microphone (smartphone, tablet, or computer)
- Reliable internet connection
- A private location where you can speak freely
- Updated web browser or the app specified by your provider

You are responsible for ensuring your technology is functional before scheduled appointments.

---

### 6. Privacy and Confidentiality

Your telehealth sessions are conducted on secure, HIPAA-compliant platforms. We use encryption and security measures to protect your information.

However, you share responsibility for privacy:
- Ensure you are in a private location during video visits
- Do not allow unauthorized individuals to observe your session
- Do not record sessions without provider consent
- Use secure networks (avoid public Wi-Fi when possible)

Your health information will be handled according to our [Notice of Privacy Practices](https://agnihealth.co/privacy).

---

### 7. Prescriptions

When clinically appropriate, your provider may prescribe medications electronically. You understand:
- Not all medications can be prescribed via telehealth
- Agni Health does not prescribe DEA-controlled substances
- Prescription availability may vary by state and pharmacy
- You are responsible for picking up prescriptions from your designated pharmacy

---

### 8. Geographic Requirements

You must be physically located in one of the following states at the time of each telehealth consultation:
- **California**
- **Louisiana**
- **Massachusetts**

If you relocate or are traveling outside these states, you must notify us. We cannot provide care while you are located outside our licensed states.

---

### 9. Your Rights

You have the right to:
- Withdraw consent and stop telehealth services at any time
- Request an in-person referral if telehealth is inadequate for your needs
- Ask questions about any aspect of your telehealth care
- Access your medical records
- File a complaint if you believe your rights have been violated

Withdrawing consent will not affect your right to future care, though in-person services would need to be obtained elsewhere.

---

### 10. Fees
