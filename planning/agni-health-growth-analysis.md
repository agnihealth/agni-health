| Metric | Hours |
|--------|-------|

### Maximum Panel Size (Solo)
```
At 3.4 hr/patient/year:
- 1,500 hrs ÷ 3.4 = ~440 patients (steady state, no growth)
- With 20% capacity for new patients: ~350 patients max
```

**⚠️ Key Finding: 1,000 patients is NOT achievable solo.** 
A solo physician hits capacity around 300-400 patients.

## 4. Key Bottlenecks for Solo Physician

### Capacity Constraints (In Order of Impact)

| Bottleneck | Hits At | Impact |
|------------|---------|--------|
| **1. Synchronous visit time** | 150-200 patients | Can't fit all follow-ups |
| **2. Async message volume** | 200-250 patients | Inbox overwhelm, delayed responses |
| **3. Urgent/acute issues** | 250-300 patients | Quality of care concerns |
| **4. Burnout risk** | 300+ patients | Unsustainable pace |
| **5. Admin overhead** | Throughout | Billing, scheduling, marketing |

### Operational Bottlenecks

| Area | Issue | Mitigation |
|------|-------|------------|
| **Scheduling** | Manual coordination | Automate with Calendly/Acuity early |
| **Intake/onboarding** | Time-consuming | Async intake forms, self-service |
| **Prescription refills** | Interrupts workflow | Batch processing times |
| **Lab follow-up** | Tracking results | Automated monitoring system |
| **Marketing** | Time away from care | Outsource or batch content creation |

### Regulatory/Legal

| Constraint | Details |
|------------|---------|
| State licensing | Currently 3 states; each new state = time + cost |
| Controlled substances | GLP-1s not controlled, but some metabolic meds are |
| Malpractice scaling | Premiums increase with patient volume |
| Corporate practice of medicine | CA has strict rules on employment structures |

## 5. Scaling Recommendations

### Timeline for Key Decisions

| Milestone | Action Required |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **50 patients** | Hire part-time virtual MA ($20-25/hr, 10-15 hrs/wk) |
| **100 patients** | Add health coach for async check-ins ($50-80K/yr) |
| **150-200 patients** | Begin recruiting NP/PA (lead time: 2-3 months) |
| **250 patients** | NP/PA must be onboarded; solo not sustainable |
| **400+ patients** | Second physician or physician oversight model |

### Recommended Scaling Model

**Option A: NP/PA Leverage Model**
- Dr. Japa: Complex cases, new patient consults, oversight
- NP/PA: Routine follow-ups, stable patients
- Health coach: Async check-ins, lifestyle coaching
- Ratio: 1 MD can oversee 2-3 NPs = 800-1,200 patients

**Option B: Physician Partnership**
- Recruit second metabolic health physician
- Split panel, shared call coverage
- Better for physician autonomy, higher cost

## 6. Summary & Recommendations

### Key Numbers
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Solo physician ceiling | **300-400 patients** |
| Time to 1,000 patients | **3-3.5 years** (with team) |
| Revenue at 1,000 (mid-market) | **$1.9-2.4M gross** |
| Net margin at scale | **$1.0-1.6M** |
| First hire needed | **~50 patients / $100K ARR** |

### Strategic Recommendations

1. **Pricing**: Start at $199/mo (mid-market). Premium positioning justified by cultural specialization and physician-led care. Can test $249 with early adopters.

2. **First 100 patients**: Focus on systems, not speed. Build scalable intake, async workflows, and content library.

3. **Plan hiring early**: Start recruiting NP/PA at 150 patients (before you need them). Credentialing takes 60-90 days.

4. **Consider group visits**: Metabolic health education via group video (4-6 patients) could 3x throughput for certain touchpoints.

5. **Retention > acquisition**: At $199/mo, reducing churn from 20% to 15% = $120K additional revenue at 1,000 patients.
