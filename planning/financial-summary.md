## Executive Summary

- **Breakneck ramp to capacity.** At 5 new patients/week, the panel fills to 100 in just **5 months** — an aggressive but achievable intake pace for a focused telehealth practice. The hard limit isn't marketing; it's physician bandwidth.

- **Break-even is Day 1.** With only $2,000/month in fixed overhead, the practice is cash-flow positive from the very first patient. Month 1 alone generates $16,500 in revenue against $2,000 in costs — a 14.5× coverage ratio.

- **MRR at full capacity: $48,500/month.** Once the Executive tier opens at Month 6 and the panel stabilizes, the blended MRR per patient rises from $400 to $485. The top 10 Executive patients ($1,250/mo each) contribute $12,500 of that — 26% of MRR from just 10% of the panel.

- **Year 1 revenue: $514,250.** The ramp-up phase (Months 1–5) generates $162,500; the full-panel steady state (Months 6–12) adds $351,750. Net profit after fixed costs: ~$490,250 — effectively physician take-home pre-tax.

- **Year 2 revenue: $603,000.** A fully mature panel operating 12 months at capacity with zero growth lift. The 17% Y1→Y2 increase is purely structural — the ramp months in Y1 are replaced by full-capacity months in Y2.

- **Two-year cumulative: $1,117,250.** A solo physician clearing over $1M in 24 months with minimal overhead is the core thesis of concierge telehealth. The main lever to accelerate: reduce the 5% monthly churn — dropping it to 3% would increase steady-state MRR by ~$800–1,000/month.
